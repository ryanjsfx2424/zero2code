Zero2Code Computational Astrophysics Summer Program 2024

Ryan Jeffrey Farber, PhD, has 1-2 openings for astrophysics research experiences this summer. Project 1: BLACK HOLE ACCRETION: A COMPARATIVE STUDY. Application Deadline: July 1st, 2024. Background: A star's life exists as a constant competition between gravitational collapse, and the production of pressure gradients in resistance. For the most massive stars, gravity wins forming an object so compact that even light cannot escape its gravitational influence: a black hole. Previous work: Last summer, I supervised a student who performed her own 2D computational fluid dynamic simulations of the Keplerian subsonic regimes of black hole accretion disks. She compared the accretion rates predicted by astrophysical codes FLASH and Athena. Currently, my student is writing up the results of her research for publication as a Research Note of the American Astronomical Society, as she is about to begin an internship this summer with the European Space Agency. Additionally, she was accepted for a masters program at Dundee University to study the light curves produced by black hole accretion disks. Example Project: I am looking for a student interested in significantly extending our previous work either by i) comparing additional astrophysical codes, such as Ramses, Athena++, Arepo, Gizmo, etc., ii) studying additional astrophysical regimes for black hole accretion: supersonic, magnetized, and/or relativistic, or iii) a more self-consistent treatment of the onset of accretion, by simulating the Roche overflow of a companion star. In this project, you will investigate black hole accretion by running your own state-of-the-art computational fluid dynamic simulations. Project 2: OTHER POTENTIAL PROJECTS I can also offer supervision of projects to study turbulent radiative mixing layers, cloud-crushing, galaxy formation & evolution.
